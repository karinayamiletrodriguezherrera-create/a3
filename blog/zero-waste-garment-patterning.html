Zero-Waste Garment Patterning

By Pattern Engineer • 8 Min Read

Standard apparel manufacturing discards 15% to 20% of fabric as cutting scrap waste during garment production. **Zero-Waste Patterning** uses algorithmic tessellation to interlock pattern pieces seamlessly across the fabric bolt, eliminating cutting waste completely. This guide explores zero-waste patterning.

1. Algorithmic Tessellated Pattern Layouts

Designing pattern shapes like puzzle pieces ensures 100% of the organic cotton fabric bolt is utilized in the garment construction.

2. Integrated Pocket & Trim Panel Design

Scraps that would otherwise be discarded are incorporated as pocket linings, hood facings, and internal reinforced seam tapes.
